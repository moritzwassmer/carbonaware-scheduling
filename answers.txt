General Note: We used 3 nodes including the control plane, but allowed the control plane to work on workloads as well.

1) Since this is an artificial scenario, you can simply assume that each active pod 
consumes 200W of electricity. As workloads run 40s on average and we have 
180 workloads in total, the expected overall energy usage is40s * 180 * 200W 
= 400 Wh. What is the carbon footprint? 

Assuming the mentioned "intensity" is defined as g CO2eq/kWh (since not explicitely specified) the avg. carbon footprint is:
Energy consumption: 400 Wh = 0.4 kWh
avg. Footprint normal: 0.4 kWh * 290.72 g CO2eq/kWh = 116.29 g C02eg
avg. Footprint carbon: 0.4 kWh * 212.86 g CO2eq/kWh = 85.14 g C02eg
